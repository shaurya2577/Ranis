Rani's Legacy

In the 1960s, our grandmother Rani worked at a textile mill in Gujarat. After her shift, she'd gather fabric scraps—pieces too small or imperfect for production—and bring them home. At her hand-crank Singer, she turned discards into garments for her daughters: blouses from silk selvage, skirts from miscut yardage, jackets lined with sample swatches.

Rani never called herself a designer. She was practical. But her eye was sharp: she understood how to balance a narrow shoulder with a wide cuff, how to mix a traditional kurta silhouette with Western tailoring. She believed nothing beautiful should be wasted.

We are her three granddaughters. Today we design from California, New York, and London, but our garments are still sewn in the same small workshop in Gujarat where Rani once stitched. We work with the families of artisans who knew her, using natural-dye cottons, handloom silks, and organic wools. Each piece bridges her generation's thrift and our generation's questions: How do we dress with intention? How do we honor both heritage and the present?

Every Rani's garment is small-batch—often made to order, sometimes one of three. We don't chase trends. We make clothing meant to last, to be altered, to be passed down. Rani taught us that.

How We Make

Our garments are sewn in a family-run workshop in Gujarat, India—the same town where Rani once worked. We partner with second- and third-generation textile artisans who specialize in hand-dyeing, block printing, and fine tailoring. Production batches are small, typically 3–10 pieces per style, which allows us to source deadstock and organic fabrics without waste.

All workers are paid above fair-trade wages and work standard hours. We visit the workshop twice a year and maintain transparent communication year-round. Fabrics are chosen for longevity: natural fibers, colorfast dyes, reinforced seams. Our lead times reflect the care required—most pieces take 3–5 weeks from order to completion.

We don't use synthetic fabrics, chemical dyes, or fast-production methods. If a garment doesn't meet our standards, it's altered or remade, never discarded. Rani's philosophy remains: make less, make better, waste nothing.

Questions

In-stock items ship within 3–5 business days. Made-to-measure pieces take 4–6 weeks (includes tailoring and international shipping from our Gujarat workshop). We'll send progress updates and tracking once your order ships.

Custom-made pieces are final sale due to their personalized nature. However, if there's a measurement error on our part, we'll remake the garment at no cost. In-stock items can be returned within 14 days in unworn condition for store credit.

After you submit a custom order request, we'll send a detailed measurement guide with photos and video. If possible, have someone help you measure. Wear fitted clothing (not bulky layers) and measure over your usual undergarments. We also offer virtual fitting consultations via video call.

We work exclusively with natural fibers: organic cotton, handloom silk, linen, and wool. Many fabrics are deadstock (surplus from larger mills) or naturally dyed using traditional methods. Each product listing specifies fabric content and origin.

Most pieces should be dry cleaned or hand washed in cold water with gentle detergent. Naturally dyed items may require special care (included with your order). Avoid direct sunlight for extended periods to preserve color. Detailed care instructions come with every garment.
